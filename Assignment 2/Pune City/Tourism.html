Discover Pune

Aga Khan Palace

The Aga Khan Palace in Pune is a historical monument known for its architectural elegance and significant role in India's freedom struggle. Built in 1892 by Sultan Muhammad Shah Aga Khan, it was originally designed as a lavish residence. The palace became notable as the place where Mahatma Gandhi, along with his wife Kasturba Gandhi and other leaders, was imprisoned during the Quit India Movement in 1942. Today, the Aga Khan Palace is a museum that showcases the life and contributions of Mahatma Gandhi and serves as a memorial to the freedom movement. It is an important cultural and historical landmark, surrounded by beautiful gardens.
